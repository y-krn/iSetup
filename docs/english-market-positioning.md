# English Market Positioning

## Direction

iSetup should expand into English-speaking iPhone setup communities while preserving the existing Japanese product experience.

The strategy is:

> Japanese base + English expansion.

English growth is an additional market strategy, not a replacement for Japanese positioning, Japanese SEO, Japanese sharing flows, or Japanese legal pages.

The core positioning is:

> Discover real iPhone setups and the apps behind them.

iSetup should not compete as a generic screenshot gallery. The differentiator is that each screenshot becomes a setup profile: apps, widgets, colors, theme, and screen type are extracted and shown.

## One-Line Definition

Use this when explaining the product:

> iSetup is a gallery of real iPhone home screens and lock screens, with the apps, widgets, colors, and themes automatically detected.

Shorter brand line:

> Real iPhone setups, decoded.

Japanese positioning should remain natural rather than directly translated from English. A useful Japanese direction is:

> iOSホーム画面・ロック画面を共有しよう。使っているアプリやウィジェットも自動解析。

## Primary Audience

Start with English-speaking users who already care about iPhone customization:

- iPhone home screen setup enthusiasts.
- Minimal and aesthetic setup users.
- Productivity setup users.
- Widget and Widgy users.
- Lock screen widget users.
- People who browse setup inspiration and want to recreate it.

Initial target regions:

- United States
- United Kingdom
- Canada
- Australia
- Singapore
- English-speaking online communities regardless of country

Do not start with broad worldwide localization. Begin with English copy, English sharing flows, English metadata, and English category language while keeping the Japanese experience intact.

## Language Strategy

The product should support two market tracks:

- Japanese track: keep the current natural Japanese product experience, Japanese sharing copy, and Japanese SEO potential.
- English track: add English-facing copy, sharing flows, metadata, and category language for overseas discovery.

Avoid replacing all Japanese UI with English. Instead, make language choices deliberate by surface:

- `/` language behavior should be decided separately. It may remain Japanese, become locale-aware, or route users to `/ja` and `/en` later.
- `/ja` and `/en` are likely future routes, but full i18n does not need to be the first implementation step.
- Shared post URLs can stay common while the rendered copy and metadata become locale-aware later.
- Legal pages should eventually have Japanese and English versions. Decide separately whether Japanese is the controlling version.
- Copy should be localized, not directly translated.

## User Desire

The important user desire is not only "show me nice screenshots."

English-speaking setup communities often care about:

- What apps are those?
- What widgets are those?
- How can I recreate this?
- What is in the dock?
- Is this a real setup or just a mockup?
- What kind of wallpaper/colors make this work?

iSetup should answer those questions better than a plain image post.

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Real Setups

Use:

- Real iPhone setups
- Real home screens and lock screens
- Setups from real people

Avoid implying that setups are templates, generated mockups, or official Apple examples.

### 2. Decoded Setup Details

Use:

- Apps behind the setup
- Widgets, colors, and theme
- Automatically detected
- Setup profile

This is the main product difference.

### 3. Recreate and Discover

Use:

- Find setups you can recreate
- Discover apps and widgets people actually use
- Get home screen inspiration

This is stronger than saying "AI screenshot analyzer."

## Category Language

Start with these English-facing categories and filters:

- Minimal
- Aesthetic
- Productivity
- Dark
- Light
- Clean Dock
- Lock Screen Widgets
- Home Screen
- Everyday Setup
- Focus Mode
- Widgy

Avoid relying only on "Minimal." It is subjective and can trigger unhelpful debates. Use adjacent categories such as Clean, Everyday, Productivity, and Aesthetic.

## Initial Growth Motions

### 1. Make Sharing English-Ready

When a user posts successfully, the sharing prompt should support English copy and produce a clean public link with OGP.

Priority:

- X share
- Threads share
- Copy link
- Reddit-friendly title/copy later

### 2. Seed English-Friendly Posts

Initial gallery should include:

- Minimal setups
- Productivity setups
- Dark setups
- Aesthetic setups
- Lock screen widget examples
- Everyday/default-ish setups

Avoid making the gallery look too elite or design-only. Everyday setups lower posting anxiety.

### 3. Make App Detail Pages SEO-Relevant

`/apps/[name]` can become an English discovery surface:

- iPhone setups using Messages
- iPhone setups using Widgetsmith
- iPhone setups using Widgy
- iPhone setups using Google Calendar

The page should eventually explain that users can find setups where each app appears.

### 4. Observe Communities Before Posting

Relevant communities:

- Reddit iOS setup communities
- X/Threads iPhone customization posts
- Widget/Widgy communities
- Wallpaper and icon pack creators

Do not spam links. Start by sharing examples and asking for feedback.

## Positioning to Avoid

Avoid:

- Pinterest for iPhone home screens
- Share your iPhone screenshots
- AI-powered iOS screenshot analyzer
- A gallery for iOS home screens

These are either too generic, too feature-oriented, or too easy for existing platforms to replace.

## Product Implications

To support this positioning, prioritize:

- Preserve Japanese top-level copy or provide a clear Japanese route.
- Add English top-level copy where it helps overseas discovery.
- Support Japanese and English OGP metadata over time.
- Support Japanese and English post-success sharing prompts.
- Stronger "apps behind this setup" presentation on post detail pages.
- Japanese and English category/filter labels.
- Clear upload warnings about personal information.
- Legal/privacy review before intentional EU targeting.

## Legal and Policy Notes

Before actively targeting overseas users:

- Review Japanese and English Terms and Privacy Policy.
- Decide whether the Japanese legal text is the controlling version.
- Confirm Apple/iPhone/iOS/App Store wording does not imply affiliation.
- Explain that uploaded screenshots are processed by Google Gemini API.
- Explain that public screenshots may contain personal information if users fail to review them.
- Provide a clear takedown/contact path.
- Consider GDPR and DSA implications before explicitly targeting EU users.

This document is product strategy, not legal advice.
